About Me

I'm a mechanical engineer who enjoys turning ideas into real, working things, whether that means designing parts in SolidWorks, coding up control systems on an Arduino, or troubleshooting a custom PC build. My background blends mechanical design, software development, and electronics, so I naturally gravitate toward projects that sit at the intersection of all three. I've worked on everything from small form factor PC cases to robotic prototypes, and I'm always looking for ways to make a design more efficient, elegant, and easy to build.

Outside of work and school projects, I spend a lot of time experimenting, testing new CAD workflows, tinkering with Linux systems, or exploring how hardware and software can mesh more seamlessly. I like working in teams where ideas move quickly, feedback is open, and everyone contributes something unique. More than anything, I'm motivated by building things that are both technically sound and thoughtfully designed.
